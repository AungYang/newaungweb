OVERVIEW

Transparents is a student team from my usability and information architecture class that prototyped an app we call AcademiaHub. Our quarter-long project focused on a "anything kids" theme that looks at developing ideas to help children.

Analogous to engineers having a belt around their waist with different types of tools, our app integrates multiple useful features to vastly enhance a student's academic experience in highschool. Through the 5-week sprint, we prototyped two features:

  • Major Planner → allows users to determine what to major in based on factors like interests and choice of career field
  • GPA Calculator → allows users to see their overall and term GPAs, along with CRUD actions for their grades and assignments for any chosen term.

A little bit about our team name; it is inspired by Don Norman and his ideas about cognitive artifacts being "transparent." The more transparent a representation is, the more natural it becomes - thus our team strives to design our app such that users don't think about the representation but rather what it represents.

Problem Statement: Many highschool students do not take advantage of academic tools and generally are unaware of embedding technology in their daily lives to achieve cognitive scaffolding. More specifically, our clients aren't sure of the major(s) they want to pursue and do not fully understand how their grades are determined.

Initial Proposed Solution: Create an interface that allows for access to our two features. The first feature will list majors based on users' answers and the second feature will allow users to see their GPA (overall, terms, & classes).

Competitor Analysis In the process of building our Figma prototype, we wanted to prototype features that surpass the current ones on the market. We picked Me3 and Grades as products that we can look to as reference while also designing features that does even more for the user.

Product Pros Cons
Me3 Informs users about certain majors and careers with the click of a button and allows the user to save suggested majors to their account Question set is way too long and the major algorithm needs improvement - no context to pictures used
Grades Users can use the app to track their grade in the class in addition to a "what-if" function Same GPA scale system applied to all classes. On weighted class system: can’t add sub-assignments; can only change overall score for category


Based on the cons of these apps, we aimed to improve user experience by prototyping improved features and integrating them to our app. In our Figma prototype, we:

  • Essentially merged two features such that it's accessible under one app
  • Avoided long questionnaires to battle fatigue effects
  • Enabled the functionality of not only saving majors, but also being able to access resources associated with that major
  • Added a "suggested courses" page to help students pick better classes in school
  • Ensured the GPA Calculator addresses weighted/unweighted (AP vs regular) classes
  • Ensured the GPA Calculator allows users to configure the cut off for grades (93 vs 92 for an A-)
  • Ensured the GPA Calculator allows users to choose whether their class uses the +/- system


BETA to Final After we delivered our BETA prototype, we conducted user testing remotely. The trade-off of performing testing in the midst of a pandemic is that we do not get to see a tester's facial expressions that may convey signs of confusion when interacting with our UI, but it does come with the benefit of speed and cheaper cost. We sent out a Figma prototype link to another group in the same class along with a Google Form so that we can intake their open-ended responses. After we received feedback, we improved our prototype and made additional design decisions and implementations. Listed below briefly summarizes the changes made from BETA to final:

Major Planner
  • Added more questions
    • Before: simplistic, lacks depth, and too short
    • After: made the question set more intricate, tailored towards interests and careers
  • Added a back button during the screening process
    • Before: couldn't change answer and had to finish the assessment before retrying
    • After: can change answer in case user makes a mistake
  • Got rid of unnecessary bolding
    • Before: everything was bolded so nothing stood out
    • After: carefully selected which elements should be bolded to support visual design principle
  • Revised inconsistent buttons
    • Before: some buttons had box-shadow and some were bigger in size which wasn't consistent
    • After: made button sizes the same for a more consistent visual layout
GPA Calculator
  • Added "swipe left" function for additional affordances (edit/delete)
    • Before: added classes/assignments couldn't be edited nor deleted
    • After: additional affordances are implemented while minimizing clutter
  • Changed the color of the container that shows GPA from red to green
    • Before: red gives off an angry, negative air (color psychology)
    • After: using green makes it pleasant, at the very least neutral
  • Displayed both unweighted GPA and weighted GPA
    • Before: GPA was assumed to be weighted, thus didn't have information on unweighted
    • After: now shows both types of GPA
  • Added labels on back buttons
    • Before: only had an arrow which made it confusing for users to know where it was leading back to
    • After: the label helps clarify where users are headed
